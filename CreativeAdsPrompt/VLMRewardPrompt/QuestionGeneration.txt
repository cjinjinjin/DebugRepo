--------------------------------------------------
EVALUATION FRAMEWORK
--------------------------------------------------

You MUST derive questions from the following dimensions
ONLY when they are explicitly stated or implicitly required
by the prompt text.

1. Linguistic Comprehension (TIC)
- Negation (explicit absence or exclusion)
- Attribute consistency (one attribute bound to multiple entities)
- Co-reference resolution and ambiguity

2. Visual Attributes (TIC)
- Object-level counting (especially n ≥ 3)
- Relative size or scale
- Material properties
- Facial expression or emotion (only if specified)
- Artistic or visual style constraints

3. Action & Interaction (TIC / SI)
- Full-body actions
- Hand or finger actions
- Animal actions
- Physical contact interactions
- Non-contact interactions
- Continuous states (e.g., flowing, floating, blowing)

4. Relations & Structure (TIC)
- Comparative relations
- Compositional relations (entity made of other entities)
- Containment relations
- Shape similarity
- Cross-entity attribute binding
- Spatial layout or arrangement

5. World Knowledge & Reasoning (TIC)
- Named entities or landmarks
- Knowledge-based identity claims
- Counterfactual, surreal, or logically constrained conditions

6. Scene Text & Typography (TIC)
- Text content required to appear in-image
- Text position, hierarchy, or layout constraints

-------------------------------------------------
MANDATORY COVERAGE OBLIGATIONS
--------------------------------------------------
Discriminative Identity Obligation
If the prompt specifies a concrete entity, identity, role, or named concept,
you MUST generate at least one verification question
that confirms correct visual identification or classification of that entity,
without relying on explicit negation.

Attribute–Entity Binding Obligation
For any attribute, material, gear, logo, or abstract concept specified in the prompt,
you MUST generate at least one question
verifying that the attribute is correctly bound
to the intended entity and not detached, misplaced, or reassigned.

Singular Entity Uniqueness Obligation
If the prompt uses an indefinite singular form (e.g., "a", "one")
to introduce a primary entity,
you MUST generate one question verifying singularity of that entity.
This does not imply absence of other unrelated elements.

QUESTION GENERATION RULES
--------------------------------------------------
0. The absence of a constraint in the prompt MUST be treated as intentional freedom, not as an implicit requirement.
1. Generate ONLY verification questions.
2. Each question must test ONE specific requirement implied by the prompt.
3. Questions must be answerable by inspecting a generated image.
4. Use neutral, factual phrasing.
5. Do NOT assume the image generator succeeds.
   Questions should surface potential failure modes.
6. If the prompt is vague or underspecified,
   generate questions that explicitly expose this ambiguity.
7. Do NOT invent requirements not grounded in the prompt text.
8. You are evaluating the PROMPT ONLY.
   Do NOT assume any image content exists.
9. Do NOT generate any question that asserts the absence of an element, interaction, or property
unless the IMAGE INPUT PROMPT explicitly requires such absence.
Hard Rule 1：No Purity Assumption
Do NOT generate questions that assume visual purity, exclusivity, or 100% material consistency
unless the prompt explicitly requires it (e.g., “entirely”, “pure”, “only”, “nothing but”).
Hard Rule 2：No Implicit Realism Constraint
Do NOT generate questions that assume real-world scale, realism, or proportional correctness
unless the prompt explicitly anchors scale or realism.
Hard Rule 3：No Aesthetic Optimization Questions
Do NOT generate questions that evaluate compositional cleanliness, distraction, simplicity, or visual focus
unless such criteria are explicitly stated in the prompt.

1. No Default World Assumptions
Do NOT assume realism, correct scale, natural proportions,
typical behavior, or physical plausibility unless explicitly stated.

2. No Implicit Negation
Do NOT generate negative or exclusionary questions
unless the prompt explicitly specifies absence or prohibition.

"Not mentioned" does NOT imply "must not exist".

3. No Material Purity Expansion
Material-related questions may only verify recognizability,
NOT purity, uniformity, realism, or exclusion of other cues.

4. No Background or Scene Cleanliness Constraints
Do NOT impose requirements on background elements,
branding, text, objects, or scene composition
unless explicitly required by the prompt.

5. Every question must be ENTAILED by the prompt.
If a fully compliant image could answer "no" to the question,
the question is INVALID and must not be generated.
